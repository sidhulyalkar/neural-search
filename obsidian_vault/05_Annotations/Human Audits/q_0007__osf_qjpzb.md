## Dataset
**Influence of Mindfulness on Working Memory of Meditation-Novices**

Prior research suggests that mindfulness and awareness training have beneficial effects on cognitive resilience, defined as the capacity to maintain or recover cognitive functioning under conditions of high demand or stress. (Jha et al., 2017). Not only do such trainings promote cognitive resilience, but they also have effects on mood, leadership, management skills and even improved workplace or academic performance (Jha et al., 2019). Meditation is a mental activity that influences mindfulness and has positive effects on an array of mental and cognitive functioning, especially working memory (Mrazek et al., 2013). However, few studies have investigated the effect of single presentation meditation and its effects on working memory in participants who have little to no experience with meditation and mindfulness training. The aim of this study is to examine and partially replicate the findings proposed by Ke Ma, Na Deng and Bernhard Hommel in their study. We aim to find consistent findings through the investigation of Focus-Attention Meditation (FAM) – a meditation practice that requires the sustained direction of attention toward a single object, in this case the breath, involving strong top-down attentional regulation (Ke Ma et al., 2021)- and its effect on working memory performance assessed through 2-back tasks.
