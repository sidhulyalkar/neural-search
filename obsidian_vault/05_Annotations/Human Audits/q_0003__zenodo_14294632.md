## Dataset
**Supplementary data for: PseudoSorter: A Self-Supervised Spike Sorting Approach Applied to Reveal Tau-Induced Reductions in Neuronal Activity**

<p><span>Supplementary example data for the work presented in "<em>PseudoSorter: A Self-Supervised Spike Sorting Approach Applied to Reveal Tau-Induced Reductions in Neuronal Activity</em>".&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><strong>Abstract:&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><span>Microelectrode arrays (MEAs) permit recordings with high electrode counts, thus generating complex datasets which would benefit from precise neuronal spike sorting for meaningful data extraction. Nevertheless, conventional spike sorting methods face limitations in recognising diverse spike shapes. Here, we introduce <em>PseudoSorter</em>, which employs self-supervised learning techniques, a density-based pseudo-labelling strategy, and an iterative fine-tuning process to enhance spike sorting accuracy. Through benchmarking, we demonstrate the superior performance of <em>PseudoSorter</em> compared to other spike sorting algorithms before applying <em>PseudoSorter</em> on MEA recordings from hippocampal neurons exposed to subneuronal concentrations of monomeric Tau as a model for Alzheimer's disease. Our results unveil that Tau diminishes the firing rate of a subset of neurons, which complement our findings observed using conventional electrophysiology analysis, and demonstrate that <em>PseudoSorter</em>&rsquo;s high accuracy and throughput make it a valuable tool for studying neurodegenerative diseases, enhancing our understanding of their underlying mechanisms, as well as for therapeutic drug screening.</span></p>
