## Dataset
**When Plants Respond: Electrophysiology and ML for Green Monitoring Systems**

<p>Living plants, while contributing to ecological balance and&nbsp;climate regulation, also function as natural sensors capable of transmitting&nbsp;information about their internal physiological states and surrounding&nbsp;conditions. This rich source of data provides potential for applications&nbsp;in environmental monitoring and precision agriculture. With integration&nbsp;into biohybrid systems, we establish novel channels of (physiological) signal&nbsp;flow between living plants and artificial devices. We equipped Hedera&nbsp;helix with a plant-wearable device called PhytoNode, to continuously&nbsp;record the plant&rsquo;s electrophysiological activity. We deployed plants in&nbsp;an uncontrolled outdoor environment to map electrophysiological patterns&nbsp;to environmental conditions. Over five months, we collected and&nbsp;analyzed data using state-of-the-art and automated machine learning&nbsp;(AutoML). Our classification models achieve high performance, reaching&nbsp;macro F1-scores of up to 95% in binary tasks. AutoML approaches&nbsp;outperformed manual tuning and selecting subsets of statistical features&nbsp;further improved accuracy. Our biohybrid living system monitors the&nbsp;electrophysiology of plants in harsh, real-world conditions. This work&nbsp;advances scalable, self-sustaining, and plant-integrated living biohybrid&nbsp;systems for sustainable environmental monitoring.</p>
