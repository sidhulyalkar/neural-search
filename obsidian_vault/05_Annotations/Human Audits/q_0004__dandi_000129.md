## Dataset
**MC_RTT: macaque motor cortex spiking activity during self-paced reaching**

This dataset contains sorted unit spiking times and behavioral data from a Macaque rhesus performing a self-paced reaching task, used to demonstrate superior arm-movement decoding with a new, unsupervised-learning algorithm. In the experimental task, the subject reached between targets randomly selected from an 8x8 grid without gaps or pre-movement delay intervals. Neural activity was recorded from an electrode array implanted in the primary motor cortex. Finger position, cursor position, and target position were also recorded. Provided as part of the Neural Latents Benchmark (https://neurallatents.github.io) and described in Makin et al. (2018).
